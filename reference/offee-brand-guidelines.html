OFFEE BRAND IDENTITY

OFFEE is a modern hospitality-workspace brand built for hybrid professionals, remote builders, and creative minds. This document preserves our core design principles, spatial values, and visual assets, ensuring consistency from local pilots to global footprints.

01 — SPATIAL PHILOSOPHY

The Product is the Environment

Our brand succeeds when our spaces validate a professional's day. Understanding how our brand values transition from ideas to physical realities is paramount.

Warmth & Comfort

OFFEE is intentionally styled to avoid standard corporate coldness. By choosing natural wood panels, deep forest greens, and soft architectural lighting, we prioritize organic visual comfort that welcomes extended stays.

Psychological Legitimacy

Our check-in system translates payment into spatial confidence. Guests aren't "café squatting"—they own their workspace access window, allowing them to focus fully without performance anxiety or guilt.

Separation & Focus

We build a third place that serves as a deliberate transition between home and work. Every furniture placement, soundproofing measure, and power source acts as a silent partner in the user's daily progress.

05 — F&B SERVEWARE & PACKAGING

Welcome Handshake Presentation

Although focus is the main product of OFFEE, the welcome beverage is our physical handshake. Its presentation must reflect simplicity, quality, and quiet confidence.

OFFEE debossed ceramic serveware on warm walnut table Asset Mockup Ref: 01

Ceramic & Textures

Our standard welcome cup is a heavy, custom matte-glazed ceramic in neutral stone-gray. The modern 'OFFEE' wordmark is debossed cleanly on the front surface. We completely avoid glossy coatings, cheap plastics, or vibrant white porcelain, favoring organic earthiness.

Packaging Aesthetics

For off-the-shelf goods and filters, we use unbleached organic kraft paper bags. Sealings are handled with a minimal black ink stamp showing the custom architectural "FF" ligature. No flashy stickers, color prints, or high-gloss plastics allowed.

06 — ACOUSTIC SOUNDSCAPING

Aural Architecture & Playlists

Visual warmth is incomplete without auditory peace. Decibel management and curated background layers are strictly monitored to foster productive concentration.

≤ 45 dB
Focus Area

Equivalent to library silence. Voice notes or calls are strictly prohibited.

≤ 60 dB
Collaboration Area

Equivalent to soft ambient chatter. Quiet brainstorming is welcomed.

≤ 50 dB
General Floor

A gentle hum of background music mixed with organic key-clicks.

Daypart Playlists Rhythms

Background music must be acoustic, organic, or atmospheric. Vocals are strictly limited to non-English or highly atmospheric layers to prevent semantic distraction.

  • Morning (09:00 - 12:00): Soft ambient piano, fingerstyle acoustic guitar, minimalist organic loops.
  • Afternoon (12:00 - 17:00): Low-frequency warm lo-fi beats, ambient coffee-house hum, cozy chillhop loops.
  • Evening (17:00 - 21:00): Cozy soft jazz, elegant bossa nova chords, warm visual lounge ambient tracks.

08 — STAFF ATTIRE & SERVICE ETHICS

The Invisible Host

OFFEE hosts are not waiters; they are facilitators of productivity. Their visual presentation and physical interactions must balance courtesy with quiet restraint.

Host Uniform Standards

Our facilitators wear lightweight, textured organic linen shirts in moss green or slate gray, paired with robust raw canvas utility aprons in a warm sand tone. Clean, minimal brass pins representing the architectural "FF" ligature are worn on the left chest.

Physical Rhythms & Flow

Hosts observe the floor silently. The welcome beverage is delivered discretely within 15 minutes of check-in, placed softly to the right of the guest's laptop with a quiet nod—never initiating conversational small talk unless addressed first.

09 — STOREFRONT & FACADE SYSTEM

Brushed Brass exterior Ident

Our facade represents our transition from the outer noise to quiet focus. It utilizes brushed metals, dark plaster, and frosted glass boundaries.

Brushed Brass Signage

The primary street sign is cut from heavy, solid brushed brass plate, back-lit with extremely warm halo LED bulbs (2200K). Never use glossy neon tubes, flashing screens, or cold white acrylic panels.

Storefront transparency

Large scale window panes must remain transparent to let passersby feel the warm wood interior. Frosting patterns are only applied as a subtle 15cm geometric band to protect seating intimacy at desk-level.

Materials & Facades

Exterior walls are finished with rough, textured charcoal stucco or warm red brickwork. This structural organic contrast alerts the customer's sensory systems that they are entering a place of organic texture.

11 — PHYSICAL SPACE PRINCIPLES

Interior Design Standards

The OFFEE brand lives in physical interactions. Every partner cafe or flagship space must adhere to these design directives to maintain sensory consistency.

💡

Lighting Guidelines

Warm Edison-style pendants are mandatory. Harsh fluorescents are strictly forbidden. The light temperature must remain consistently warm (2700K - 3000K), creating a late-afternoon mood at all times.

🌳

Material Strategy

Integrate natural wood finishes, upholstered seating, and dense plant clusters as natural dividers. Avoid bare concrete, bright glossy metals, or synthetic color accents. The texture must feel organic.

☕

The Welcome Counter

The coffee counter is visually highlighted near the entrance. It acts as our welcome station—reinforcing that while focus is the product, a premium filter coffee or chai is the welcome handshake.

14 — SPATIAL INTEGRITY RULES

Brand Protection Standards

Maintaining the sensory identity of OFFEE is vital. These strict guidelines prevent dilution of our physical and visual brand elements.

✓ Brand Directives (DO)
✓
Always position the architectural FF ligature as a premium brand asset with ample breathing room.
✓
Use natural partition elements like wooden bookshelves, warm lighting gaps, or lush plant planters.
✓
Ensure power sockets and premium back-up routers are readily accessible at every single seat.
✓
Implement diagonal color gradients at 135 degrees to preserve spatial texture on user interfaces.
✕ Spatial Violations (DON'T)
✕
Do not stretch, outline, or scale the brand marks unevenly across print media or signboards.
✕
Never use generic office partition walls, cubicles, or institutional metal chairs inside the zone.
✕
Do not treat OFFEE as a general coffee house. Spatial access is strictly paid, focused, and verified.
✕
Avoid flat, high-contrast, pure-primary colors. The visual pallet must remain warm, muted, and cozy.
